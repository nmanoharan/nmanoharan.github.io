Privacy Policy — HealthGenie

Last updated: May 3, 2026

This policy describes how the HealthGenie prototype applications and related materials in this project handle information. HealthGenie provides preventive wellness guidance only and is not a medical device or substitute for professional care.

Who this applies to

This policy applies to users of the HealthGenie web and mobile demo clients that send data to workflows you operate (for example, n8n webhooks you configure).

What we collect

Depending on how you use the app, the following categories of information may be processed:

  • Wellness profile inputs (for example: age, height, weight, activity level, goals, diet preferences, allergies, ethnicity or cultural context, family history context, medical constraints text you enter).
  • Daily checkpoint inputs (for example: activity completion, steps, energy and sleep ratings, pain reporting text, meal adherence).
  • Technical data typical of mobile/web apps (for example: IP address, device/browser type, timestamps) as handled by your hosting providers, n8n instance, and any analytics you enable outside this repository.

How information is used

Information you submit is used to:

  • Generate onboarding summaries, diet and exercise plan outputs, checkpoint responses, and safety messaging.
  • Improve reliability of the prototype during your testing.

This repository does not include a production user database. Any persistence is configured by you (for example, n8n execution history, external databases, or local on-device storage in the mobile demo).

On-device storage (mobile demo)

The Expo mobile demo may store recent responses locally on your device (for example, to show the last plan view after closing the app). That data remains on the device unless you clear app storage or uninstall the app.

Third-party services you control

When you configure the app to call your n8n instance, inputs are transmitted to that instance. Your use of n8n is governed by your agreement with n8n and your own privacy practices. If you connect LLM providers or databases inside workflows, those providers may process data according to their policies.

Legal bases (summary)

For EU/UK concepts, processing is generally based on consent (where you present a consent step) and/or legitimate interests in operating and securing your prototype, depending on how you deploy the system. You should align this section with your actual deployment and legal advice.

Retention

Retention depends on your n8n settings, databases, and logs. This repository does not define a global retention schedule for end users.

Security

Use HTTPS endpoints, restrict access to your n8n instance, rotate credentials, and follow least-privilege practices for production deployments.

Children

HealthGenie is not directed at children under 13 (or the minimum age required in your jurisdiction).

Your choices

  • You can stop submitting data by uninstalling the app or discontinuing use of the web client.
  • You can clear local mobile storage by removing app data or uninstalling.
  • For data held in n8n or downstream systems, use the controls available in those systems.
